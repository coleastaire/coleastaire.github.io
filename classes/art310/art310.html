Stonehenge

  • Located in Southern England
  • 'Henge' is a ring bank with a ditch surrounding it
  • ain entrance precisely matches the direction of the midsummer and midwinter sunries/sunset
  • erected in 3000 BC
  • Larger stones are of Sarsen Stone, and weigh up to 44 tons each
  • Smaller stones are of Bluestone, from up to 155 miles away, and are metallic.
  • Outer ring is 108 ft in diameter
  • Likely moved the stones by either rolling, sledding, or using ancient ball bearings
  • The large stones are larger at the top in order to make it look more perfect for the human eye by correcting for the distance
  • scaffolding or ramps may have been used to stack the larger rocks
